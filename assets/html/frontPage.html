Our Leadership

Josh Barrett, JCR President

Hi, my name is Josh (they/he), I have recently graduated from a BA Geography degree, and I will be your JCR President for 2025/26.
The JCR President is a sabbatical officer (full-time paid role) who is responsible for the day-to-day running of the JCR. They represent the JCR to staff and the wider university as well as act as a trustee to the JCR.
On top of this they support the JCR Executive and Officers with their roles, and help to coordinate JCR events such as balls, formals, and Butler Day!
If you want to find out more about the JCR you can find me in the JCR Office from 9am to 5pm during weekdays, or you can email me at butler.jcr@durham.ac.uk, or scroll through the @butlerjcr Instagram page!
Feel free to get in touch if you have any questions!

Adam Johnstone, FACSO

Hi my name is Adam and I am the JCR FACSO for 2025-2026. A lot of money goes through the JCR every year and it is my job to oversee all of this. As FACSO I am in charge of making budgets for all of the year's events, helping to support students with their finances or any others issues they may have and working closely alongside the JCR President to run and organise all the great things we have planned throughout the year.
If you have any questions about the JCR or finances, then don't hesitate to send me an email at butler.facso@durham.ac.uk or come and find me in the JCR office Mon-Fri, 9am-5pm.

So, what does the JCR do?

The Junior Common Room (JCR) is the heart of undergraduate student life in college. From social events (such as balls, formals, and bar nights), to sports and societies, to welfare support, to representation in the wider university community, the JCR is here for you!

You can find our societies on the student groups page.
